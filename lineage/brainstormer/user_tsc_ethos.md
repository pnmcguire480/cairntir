Patrick developed The Stewardship Commonwealth (TSC) — a governance framework (not a government, org, or movement) built on 5 hard constraints:

1. **Limits** — Clear boundaries on extraction, risk-taking, irreversible harm
2. **Accountability** — Decisions stay attached to decision-makers, even after they move on
3. **Future consideration** — Long-term effects weighed BEFORE decisions, not retroactively
4. **Transparency** — Rules, outcomes, metrics are visible and auditable
5. **Adaptation** — Standards change when evidence says they should, not politics

Core beliefs:
- Short-term extraction is a design flaw, not a conspiracy
- What's missing isn't better intentions, it's better constraints
- Structure > personality. Constraint-based, not charisma-based.
- Voluntary adoption. Legitimacy from whether it works, not authority.
- Not utopian — just "better guardrails in this one"

**How this connects to BrainStormer:**
BrainStormer IS TSC applied to AI development. The scaffolding, severity tiers, entropy audits, session handoffs, and quality gates are all governance constraints — not product features. Frame BrainStormer as a shared standard for responsible AI-assisted development, not a SaaS product.

**How to apply:** When making product decisions for BrainStormer, filter through TSC: Does this add a constraint or remove one? Does this make responsibility harder to dodge? Is it voluntary and auditable? Patrick's instinct to go free flows directly from this — standards don't have subscriptions.
